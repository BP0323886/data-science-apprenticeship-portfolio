# 📝 Project Summary — LGBT Hate Crime Insights Dashboard (England & Wales, 2015–2024)
This project delivers an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to explore long‑term trends in police‑recorded LGBT hate crime across England and Wales (YE March 2015–2024). The dashboard integrates crime statistics with demographic and political context to support more informed interpretation of patterns over time.

The analysis highlights a sustained rise in recorded incidents through the late 2010s and early 2020s, followed by a recent decline. Sexual‑orientation‑related incidents drive most of the overall trend, while transgender‑related incidents show greater volatility. Population‑adjusted rates reveal sharper changes than raw counts alone, underscoring the importance of demographic context.

To explore broader societal influences, the dashboard incorporates national election results and referendum data. Pearson correlation and lagged year‑on‑year comparisons are used to examine how political environments align with changes in hate crime trends. These relationships are presented as exploratory and associational rather than causal.

The underlying data model follows a star‑schema design, with a dedicated analysis table enabling correct evaluation context for correlation and scatter‑based analysis. All transformations and measures are implemented using Power Query and DAX within Power BI.

The dashboard is structured across four narrative pages—trend overview, demographic context, political climate, and analytical insights—prioritising clarity, accessibility, and responsible communication of sensitive data. Ethical considerations, data limitations, and the risks of ecological fallacy are explicitly addressed throughout.

Overall, the project demonstrates how combining crime, population, and political datasets can provide a more contextualised understanding of LGBT hate crime trends, supporting evidence‑based discussion among policymakers, analysts, and stakeholders.
